Neighborhood GuideAustin, TX

Westlake

Westlake offers rolling hills, mature oak trees, and a peaceful suburban atmosphere just west of downtown Austin.

Geography and Setting

Westlake sits in the rolling hills west of central Austin, characterized by its elevated terrain and abundant mature oak trees. The neighborhood's topography creates natural privacy and scenic views throughout the area. Winding roads follow the natural contours of the land, creating a distinctly suburban feel while remaining connected to the greater Austin metropolitan area.

Architecture and Housing Character

The neighborhood features a mix of architectural styles, from traditional ranch homes to contemporary designs that take advantage of the area's natural elevation and tree cover. Many properties sit on larger lots that preserve the native Hill Country landscape, with homes often positioned to work with the natural slope of the land.

Parks and Green Space

The area maintains its connection to Austin's natural Hill Country setting through preserved green spaces and the integration of native vegetation into residential areas. The terrain and mature trees create natural corridors and pockets of greenery throughout the neighborhood.

Local Character and Lifestyle

Westlake embodies a quieter pace compared to central Austin, with tree-lined streets and a suburban atmosphere. The neighborhood's setting among the hills provides a sense of separation from the urban core while maintaining accessibility to Austin's broader amenities and cultural offerings.
