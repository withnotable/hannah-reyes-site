What's Happening in the Austin Housing Market This July

If you've been watching the Austin housing market and wondering whether now is the right time to buy or sell, you're not alone. July brings its own rhythm to real estate here — summer inventory shifts, motivated buyers on timelines, and sellers weighing whether to list before the fall slowdown. Here's what I'm seeing right now across Austin and what it means for you.

What that data doesn't capture is the texture of the market — which neighborhoods are moving fast, where buyers are finding more room to negotiate, and where sellers are still fielding multiple offers. That's where local knowledge matters most.

What's Happening in Austin Neighborhoods Right Now

Across Austin, activity varies significantly depending on the pocket you're looking at.

In East Austin, demand remains strong among buyers who want walkability and proximity to the restaurant and bar scene along East 6th and Cesar Chavez. Inventory here is limited, and well-priced homes are still moving quickly. If you're a buyer eyeing East Austin, expect competition on anything that's been updated and priced correctly.

Over in South Congress (SoCo) and South Lamar, I'm seeing a mix — some sellers pricing optimistically and sitting longer than expected, while others who priced with the current market in mind are closing smoothly. This is an area where a realistic list price makes a real difference right now.

North Loop and Hyde Park continue to attract buyers who want character and established tree cover without jumping straight into the luxury tier. Bungalows and older craftsman-style homes in these neighborhoods tend to generate genuine interest when they hit the market.

In Mueller, the planned community model keeps things relatively stable. Buyers appreciate the walkable design and access to Bartholomew District Park, and the neighborhood draws people who are thinking long-term about their lifestyle, not just their square footage.

Further north, Pflugerville and Round Rock — which I also cover for clients open to the greater Austin area — are offering more room to breathe, both in terms of lot size and price per square foot. For buyers who are flexible on location, these areas are worth a serious look right now.

What Should Austin Buyers and Sellers Do This Month?

If you're buying: July can actually be a productive time to be in the market. Some sellers have been sitting since spring and are more open to negotiation than they were a few months ago. That said, well-located homes in desirable Austin neighborhoods still don't linger. Get pre-approved, know your must-haves versus nice-to-haves, and be ready to move when something fits.

One thing I'm noticing on the ground: buyers who come in with clean, well-structured offers — even at or slightly below list — are having more success than they would have two years ago. The days of waiving every contingency and writing love letters are largely behind us.

If you're selling: Pricing is everything right now. Overpriced listings are sitting, and when they sit, buyers assume something is wrong. A home that launches at the right price and shows well will still attract serious attention. I'm also seeing that sellers who invest a little in pre-listing prep — fresh paint, clean landscaping, professional photos — are consistently outperforming comparable homes that hit the market without that extra effort.

At Ascend Group LPT, we work with sellers to build a pricing strategy based on actual comparable sales, not wishful thinking. That approach tends to result in faster closes and less back-and-forth during negotiations.

One More Thing I'm Watching

Interest rates continue to shape buyer behavior in ways that don't always show up in the headline numbers. I'm hearing from a lot of buyers who are waiting for rates to drop before committing — which is understandable — but waiting comes with its own risk if inventory tightens or prices shift. If the numbers work for your situation today, it's worth having that conversation rather than waiting for a perfect moment that may or may not arrive.
